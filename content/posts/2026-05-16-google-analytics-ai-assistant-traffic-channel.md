Google just made it official: AI-driven traffic is its own thing.

GA4 now includes an "AI Assistant" default channel group that specifically tracks referral traffic from recognized AI chatbot sources — naming ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude as the first three platforms in the group.

## Why This Matters

Until this week, traffic from AI engines was scattered across "Direct," "Referral," and "Organic Search" in most analytics setups. Custom regex filters were the only way to isolate it. That era is over.

The new default channel group means every GA4 property — without any configuration changes — will now see AI-driven traffic as a distinct line item. This is not a beta feature or an opt-in lab experiment. It is a [default channel group](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/9756891), which means Google considers AI assistant traffic structurally different from organic search, paid search, social, and every other acquisition category.
